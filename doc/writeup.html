Project Write-Up

Rationale for my design decisions

The goal of this visualization is to show the relationship between clinic/provider access and the out-of-state abortion rate. I decided to use a choropleth map of the United States with two sliders because this format makes it easy to see geographic patterns. The map helps show which regions have lower access, and the sliders let users filter states based on clinic and provider access levels.

Development process

I worked on this project by myself, and it took me around 12 hours to finish. I spent some time choosing the topic and thinking about how to represent the data in a clear and simple way.

I had trouble at first finding the correct source for the United States geographic map data. After I found the right file, I did some data cleaning, including mapping state names to FIPS codes. I then added two sliders as interactive elements. These sliders work as thresholds and highlight states whose clinic and provider access fall below the values selected by the user. I experimented with both "OR" and "AND" filtering, and in the end I chose the "AND" version because it shows states with consistently low access in both measures.
